About Me

Short Bio

I'm a Ph.D. student at KAIST with a passion for bridging artificial intelligence and complex engineering challenges—backed by over a decade of research experience—focusing on building data-driven generative models that can adapt across high-fidelity simulation domains like FEM, CFD, and multi-physics systems, with the goal of creating intelligent digital twins and automating engineering workflows such as numerical analysis, manufacturing, and materials design through the power of large language models (LLMs), ultimately aiming to contribute to solving some of the biggest challenges of our time—like the global population crisis—by enabling smarter, more scalable technologies.

Research

Main Research Areas

Deep Learning for Multi-Physics

Designing advanced neural network architectures tailored for accelerating complex simulations in heat transfer, fluid dynamics, materials, and coupled multi-physics systems.

Optimization × AI for Engineering

Integrating optimization algorithms with deep learning to tackle industrial and scientific problems such as semiconductor chamber design, turbomachinery optimization, and climate modeling.

Multi-Agent LLM Systems

Building multi-agent large language model frameworks to automate numerical simulations and optimize processes in areas like 3D printing and intelligent digital twin deployment.

Computational Mechanics

Developing and applying numerical methods to simulate fracture mechanics, thermal-fluid systems, and complex particle-based phenomena.
